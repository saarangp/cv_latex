\section*{Summary}

I recently completed my PhD in Medical Informatics at UCLA, where I developed self-supervised methods for learning representations of brain state from EEG and characterized how focal seizures evolve through thalamocortical circuits using intracranial recordings. These threads come together in a seizure world model that learns ictal dynamics as trajectories through a learned latent space, enabling counterfactual rollouts that forecast stimulation outcomes for closed-loop neurostimulation planning. I am broadly interested in representation learning for biological signals, with a focus on neural data, and in using mechanistic interpretability to understand what these models learn about physiology and how those insights can drive clinical applications. I previously worked with Meta FAIR's Brain \& AI group on multimodal neural encoding and decoding, and my work has been published at ICLR, Epilepsia, and ICASSP.
