\section*{Summary}

I am a PhD candidate in Medical Informatics at UCLA building foundation models for complex biological signals. My work centers on self-supervised learning, particularly JEPA-based architectures, applied to large-scale neural data, with the goal of learning transferable representations that generalize across tasks, cohorts, and recording conditions. Moving forward, I am focused on developing the interpretive methods to understand what these models learn about brain and behavior, with the ultimate goal of translating these insights into clinical applications. I previously interned with the Brain AI group at Meta FAIR in London working on multi-modal representation learning, and my work has been published at ICLR, Epilepsia, and ICASSP.
